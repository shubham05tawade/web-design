Completing the square

Consider the equation, x2 + 6x + 2. Factorising the given eqn is difficult as we cannot find two integer whose sum and product is 6 and 2 resp.

By Completing square method we can factorise the given eqn. Compare the given eqn with x2 + px + q.

Now add +(p/2)2 and -(p/2)2 in above eqn. In our case, it would be x2 + 6x + (6/2)2 + 2 - (6/2) = x2+ 6x + 9 -7 = 02

Arranging above eqn, we get (x + 3)2 - 7 = 0

Comparing above eqn with a2 - b2 = (a + b)(a - b) we get, x = 3 + √7 and x = -3 + √7

Arithmetic and Geometric Progression

A.P.

Consider, a sequence t1, t2, t3, t4, t5, t6, ...

To caculate nth term of such sequence then we can use below formula:

tn (nth term) = a +(2n -1)d where a = first term of sequence, d = difference between each number in sequence.

To caulculate sum of n term of such sequence then we can use below formula:

Sn (sum of n terms) = n/2 x (2a + (n-1)d)

G.P.

Consider, a sequence t1, t2, t3, t4, t5, t6, ...

To caculate nth term of such sequence then we can use below formula:

tn (nth term) = a x rn where a = first term of sequence, r = common ratio between two number in sequence.

To caulculate sum of n term of such sequence then we can use below formula:

Sn (sum of n terms) = (a(rn - 1)) / (r - 1). For r < 1, replace r - 1 with 1 - r.

Sum of first 'n' natural numbers Sn = n(n + 1) / 2

Sum of the squares of first 'n' natural numbers Sn = n(n + 1)(2n + 1) / 6

Sum of the cubes of first 'n' natural numbers Sn = [n(n + 1) / 2]2
