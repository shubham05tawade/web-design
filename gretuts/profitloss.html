Profit & Loss

Cost Price(C.P.): The price for which an article is bought is called is's cost price.

Selling Price(S.P.): The price for which an article is sold is called it's selling price.

Profit: SP - CP. If selling price is greater than cost price of an article then there is a profit.

%Profit = (Profit / CP) x 100

Loss: CP - SP. If cost price is greater than selling price of an article then there is a loss.

%Loss = (Loss / CP) x 100

Discount is an amount which is reduced from marked price by seller. The new selling price would be difference of marked price and discount.

%Disount = (Discount / Marked Price) x 100

Important Points:

If two items are sold, each at $x, one at a gain of p% and the other at a loss of p%, then there is an overall loss which is p2/100 %

If the C.P. of two items is the same and the % loss on one item is the same as the % gain on the other, then there is no net profit or loss.

Simple Interest and Compound Interest

When interest is paid only on the princiapl amount and only at the end of the term, it is called simple interest.

SI (Simple Interest) = PNR / 100 where P = principal amount, N = no. of year, r =rate of interest.

Accumulated or Maturity amount = SI + P

When interest is added back to the princiapl at regular intervals and interest for the next interval is calculatedd on the principal amount plus accumulated interest at beginning of the interval, it is called compound interest.

A(Amount) = P(1 + (r / 100))n

CI (Compund Interest(CI) = P(1 + (r / 100))n - P where P = principal amount, n = no. of years, r = rate of interest.
