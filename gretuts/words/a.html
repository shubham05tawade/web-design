Anticipate

1. to see what might happen in future and take action to prepare for it.

2. to think with pleasure and excitement about something that is going to happen.

3. to expect something.

  • Mnemonic: 'ATE' rhymes like await. await to see what might happen in future.
  • Synonyms: predict, await
Consistent

1. acting or doing something in same way over time.

2. being unchanging in nature or displaying the same standard over time.

3. showing regularity os steady continuity

  • Mnemonic: Consistent = Virat Kohli. Scoring century repeatedly.
  • Synonyms: stable, unvarying
Adopt

1. to takke someone else's child into your family and become legal parents.

2. to start to use particular method to show a particular attitude towards someone or something.

3. to formally accept a suggesion or policy by voting.

  • Mnemonic: 'OPT' in adopt stands for option which can be used.
  • Synonyms: espouse
Condemn

1. to express strong disapproval of someone or something usually for moral reasons.

2. to say officially that something is not safe enough to be used.

3. to show or suggest that someone is guilty of something.

  • Mnemonic: NA
  • Synonyms: castigate, denounce
Antagonistic

1. shwoing or feeling active opposition towards someone or something.

2. indicating resistance.

  • Mnemonic: 'Ant' sounds like aunt. Two aunts can oppose over any topic.
  • Synonyms: hostile, averse
Appeal

1. a formal request to a court or to someone in authority for a judegement.

2. a quality that makes someone or ssomehing attractive or interesting

3. an urgent and deeply felt request for money, help or info.

  • Mnemonic: Bowlers make appeal i.e. request to umpire for decision.
  • Synonyms: petition
Monologue

1. a long speech by one person during a conversation that stops other people from speaking or expressing an opinion.

2. a long speech in a play, movie etc. spoken by one actor.

  • Mnemonic: Monologue = Kartik Aaryan
  • Synonyms: soliloquy
